Meadowlark

Meadowlarks are medium-sized songbirds belonging to the Icteridae family, native to North and Central America. Known for their melodic and distinctive songs, meadowlarks are characterized by their bright yellow underparts with striking black markings on their chests, throats, and faces. Their upperparts are usually brown and mottled, providing excellent camouflage in grassy habitats where they are commonly found. Meadowlarks are ground-nesting birds, preferring open fields, meadows, and prairies with tall grasses where they forage for insects, seeds, and grains. Their flute-like calls and elaborate songs make them a delight for birdwatchers, and they are often considered iconic symbols of North American grasslands.
